Foundations of Computer Science (Spring 2016)

ENGR 3520 / Thu 16:00-18:30 / AC 318

This course explores the central object of study of Computer Science, namely the notion of computation. We're going to develop formal tools for defining what we mean by computation, through various forms of automata, all the way to Turing machines. We'll develop ways to talk about the efficiency of computations, by quantifying their time complexity. And we will examine alternative models of computations from the Turing machine, and see how they correspond to different programming paradigms: functional programming, logic programming, and object-oriented programming.
