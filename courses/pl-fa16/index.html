Course Information

Prerequisites: ENGR 2520 (can be taken concurrently, though it's not particularly recommended)

Instructor: Riccardo Pucella (send email)

Office hours: Tue 15:00-16:00 / MH 353

Textbook: There are several excellent books on Theory of Computation (the first part of the course), but they are expensive and we would be covering only a small part of their content. So instead, I settled on an inexpensive book that I will feel free to pick and choose from:

  • Tom Stuart, Understanding Computation

Programming: All programming in this course will be done using the programming language OCaml. The OCaml interpreter can be downloaded from the web site, and supports installations on most common systems. Here are a couple of reasonable books on OCaml that you might want to get your hands on if you prefer learning your programming language from a book instead of the web:

  • Hickey, Introduction to Objective Caml (PDF)
  • Downey, Monje, Think OCaml
  • Minsky, Madhavapeddy, Hickey, Real World OCaml

Grading: The final grade is based on weekly homework (60%) and two exams (20% each). All work will be done individually. Late homeworks will be penalized (10% per 24 hours) and no homework will be accepted after its solution has been discussed in class.

They will host a tutorial section every Monday 17:00-18:00 (AC 318). It is not mandatory to attend, but strongly recommended. In addition, they will hold the following office hours:

  • Sidd: Tuesday 6-9 (EH4 Lounge)
